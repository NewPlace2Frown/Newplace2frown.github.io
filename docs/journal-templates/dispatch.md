A dispatch is rooted in place and time. The shape: where you are, what you're noticing, why it matters this week. 300–700 words.

Lead with the situation, not a thesis. The dispatch's job is to put the reader where you are and let them see what you're seeing — the thinking can happen alongside the looking.

End with a small thing you don't know yet, or a question the next dispatch might answer. Avoid neat conclusions; this is a working notebook, not an essay.
