The opening should establish the article's stake — what's at issue, who it matters to, what claim is being made. One paragraph, three at most.

## A heading sets the next section

Articles can use H2 and H3 freely. Keep paragraphs to a measured length — the field-journal stylesheet caps line length at 64 characters, so over-long paragraphs will read as walls.

> Blockquotes work and are styled. Use them for sources, for found language, for things you want held apart from your own voice.

Articles are 800–3000 words. They earn that length by doing one of three things: documenting something carefully, working through an argument, or holding two ideas next to each other long enough that the friction tells you something.

Don't end with "in conclusion". End with the thing you came to say.
